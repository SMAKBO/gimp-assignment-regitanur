Ethanol

Reactions.

Ethanol is classified as a primary alcohol, meaning that the carbon its hydroxyl group attaches to has at least two hydrogen atoms attached to it as well. Many ethanol reactions occur at its hydroxyl group.


Reaction With Active Metals

The product will forms Alkoxied.
Using the reagent metals ; Na, Mg, Al, etc.
The reactivity of R-OH is 1o > 2o > 3o

Esterification

Esterification ( Ester Formation ) involves cleavage of C-OH bond of the acid and of the O-H bond of the alcohol.
Example :
CH3CH2OH + CH3CH2OOH --> CH3CH2COOCH2CH3 + H2


Acid-base chemistry

By reaction with an alkali metal such as sodium
2 CH3CH2OH + 2 Na → 2CH3CH2ONa + H2
CH3CH2OH + NaOH ⇌ CH3CH2ONa + H2O


Halogenation

CH3CH2OH + HCl → CH3CH2Cl + H2O
CH3CH2OH + SOCl2 → CH3CH2Cl + SO2 + HCl
4Cl2 + CH3CH2OH → CCl3CHO + 5 HCl
CCl3CHO + H2O → CCl3C(OH)2


Oxidation

Ethanol can be oxidized to acetaldehyde and further oxidized to acetic acid, depending on the reagents and conditions.[76] This oxidation is of no importance industrially, but in the human body, these oxidation reactions are catalyzed by the enzyme liver alcohol dehydrogenase. The oxidation product of ethanol, acetic acid, is a nutrient for humans, being a precursor to acetyl CoA, where the acetyl group can be spent as energy or used for biosynthesis.
